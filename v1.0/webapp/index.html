INS — Moral Reasoning System

Action-Based Alternating Transition System · Value-Based Argumentation Framework

The scenario

Agent Hal must decide whether and how to help Carla, a diabetic who needs insulin to survive. The tension between Hal's autonomy and Carla's need for help is expressed as a conflict of values: life vs. freedom, self vs. other.

The system models this dilemma as an AATS: states, actions, value evaluations, and structured arguments are all represented in Prolog. Arguments are evaluated under Dung (1995) abstract argumentation semantics and under the Value-Based Argumentation Framework of Bench-Capon (2003), where different audience orderings over values produce different preferred extensions.
